About Us

Since 1962, the Friends of the Abington Community Library has fostered interest in reading and library services, encouraged gifts of books and collections, and presented programs. Money from fundraisers raised by the Friends is used to purchase additional materials, equipment and facility upgrades.

Semi-annual Used Book Sales

Friends of the ACL sponsors two book sales a year (October and April) at the Clarks Summit United Methodist Church, located at the corner of West Grove Street and the Morgan Highway. Thousands of books, audio-books, movies, CDs and more are available at low prices. The Abington Community Library-Teen Leadership Committee sponsors a café featuring baked goods. Friends’ members may attend Preview Night the Friday before the sale. Members must be current with dues payments and are asked to assist with set-up before making purchases. If you’re not a member, you may join during Preview Night. Membership forms also are available at the Abington Community Library Circulation Desk. Check Library Events for Book Sale dates and contact the library if you have donations.A Board of Directors oversees the group’s activities and makes allocation decisions based upon the library’s needs.

Benefits of Joining and Dues

Annual Membership fees for Friends of the ACL are $10 per individual and $15 per family. Benefits of membership include free or discounted admission to special events, a preview of the group’s semi-annual used book sales, specific communications geared toward members only, and the knowledge that you’re helping to support a vital community asset. Please present form and fee at the library circulation desk or mail with your check payable to: Friends of the ACL, 1200 W. Grove St., Clarks Summit, PA 18411.
